Compiler Desk

Build The Doctrine Volume From Source Trails

Volume Control

Keep the official "Being Researched" status visible and separate published public statements from archival records that may carry decision or drafting evidence.

Selection Test

Candidate records should explain the administration's broad foreign-policy premises, not merely describe one regional implementation case already destined for another volume.

Source Note Path

Every candidate carries a repository, collection, identifier or search path, and a first-pass explanation for how it could support a compiler source note.

Boundary Control

NATO, Russia, Balkans, arms control, counterterrorism, global issues, and economic policy are flagged as adjacent-volume overlap unless the record is foundational doctrine or coordination.

Gate 01

Doctrine

Does the record clarify strategy, principles, priorities, or tradeoffs?

Gate 02

Decision

Does it document presidential, NSC, State, or interagency choice-making?

Gate 03

Public Case

Can the public speech be linked back to drafts, clearance, or policy memos?

Gate 04

Handoff

Does a topical volume need the detailed implementation record instead?
